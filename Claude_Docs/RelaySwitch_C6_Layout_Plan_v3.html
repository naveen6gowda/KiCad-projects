🔑 Critical Layout Insights with Corrected Relay Size

📐 The Math That Makes It Work

With K1/K2 at 12 × 21mm each, they can no longer fit side-by-side (12+12+gap = 26mm leaves only 24mm for HLK which needs 34mm). Instead:

  • HLK occupies the LEFT column: 34mm wide × 20mm tall
  • K1 + K2 stack VERTICALLY in the RIGHT column: 12mm wide × (21 + 2 + 21) = 44mm tall
  • Total width: 34mm (HLK) + 4mm gap + 12mm (relays) = 50mm ✓ exact fit!
  • K1+K2 height: 44mm — this fills nearly the entire HV zone height (~31mm available). K2 will extend slightly into the routing area, which is fine since relay pads are close to the isolation line anyway and HV traces route around them.

⚠️ Relay Orientation Matters

The G5Q-1A relay footprint is 12mm wide × 21mm tall. The 21mm dimension runs vertically. This means:

  • Relay pins are along the 12mm side (2 coil pins + 1-2 contact pins)
  • Orient K1 with contact pins facing UP (toward J3) for shortest load trace
  • Orient K2 with contact pins facing UP (toward J4)
  • Coil pins face DOWN — these connect via optocouplers (traces go down through vias to B.Cu LV zone)
  • If your KiCad footprint has relay pins along the 21mm side, rotate 90° to match this layout

💡 Why F1 and RV1 Moved to Top-Right

In your screenshot, F1 (5×20mm THT fuse) and RV1 (MOV disc) were outside the board. At those sizes, they don't fit in the gap between J1 and HLK. Instead:

  • F1 goes top-right — in the gap right of J4, above K1. It's in the mains current path: J1→F1→HLK/K1/K2
  • RV1 goes next to F1 — MOV protects across Line-Neutral, so it needs to be near both J1 L and N lines
  • Alternative: If F1 is SMD (1206) instead of THT 5×20mm cartridge fuse, it's much smaller and can fit anywhere in the HV zone. Check your schematic — is F1 a 5×20mm glass fuse or a 1206 SMD fuse?

🚨 If It's Still Too Tight — Top 3 Solutions

  1. Replace HLK-2M05 (34×20) with HLK-PM01 (25×20) — saves 9mm width instantly. 0.6W is enough for ESP32 + relay coils (200mW each). This is the single biggest win.
  2. Replace F1 THT 5×20mm fuse with SMD 1206 fuse — saves ~20mm of board space. An SMD 10A fuse (Littelfuse 0466010) works fine.
  3. Increase board to 55×45mm — 5mm extra width gives breathing room for traces and clearances. Check if your target switchboard enclosure accommodates this.
