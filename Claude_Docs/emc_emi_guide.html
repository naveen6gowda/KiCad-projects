01 What are EMI and EMC?

EMI (Electromagnetic Interference) is unwanted electromagnetic energy that disrupts the normal operation of an electronic device. It is the problem.

EMC (Electromagnetic Compatibility) is the ability of a device to function correctly in its electromagnetic environment without introducing intolerable interference to other devices. It is the goal.

EMI vs EMC relationship

Every EMI problem has three elements: a source, a coupling path, and a victim. To solve an EMI problem, you break at least one of these three links.

Two directions of EMC: Emissions — how much noise your board radiates/conducts out. Immunity (Susceptibility) — how well your board tolerates external noise.

For your products: Your RelaySwitch_C6 needs BIS CRS for India. Your CM5_MINIMA_3 with Zigbee radio (MGM240L) will need both WPC ETA (radio) and BIS CRS (IT equipment) — and EMC is a core part of both certifications.

Beyond legal compliance, EMC failures cause real functional problems: corrupted ADC readings, WiFi/Zigbee range loss, random MCU resets, and relay chatter in your relay board. Fixing EMC issues after fabrication typically costs 10–100× more than designing it right from the start.

03 How EMI is Generated

EMI comes from any circuit with changing currents or voltages. The faster the change (dI/dt or dV/dt), the more high-frequency energy is produced.

Frequency content of a digital signal (trapezoidal wave)

Key insight: the rise time (not the clock frequency) determines the highest frequency content. A 10 MHz clock with 1 ns rise time has significant energy up to 1/(π × 1ns) ≈ 318 MHz.

f_knee = 1 / (π × t_rise)

Common EMI sources on PCBs:

  • Switching regulators — the #1 offender (your HLK-2M05 in RelaySwitch_C6)
  • Clock oscillators — every harmonic is a potential emissions peak
  • Digital buses — SPI, I²C, UART, PCIe (your CM5 NVMe lane)
  • PWM signals — motor drivers, LED dimmers
  • RF transmitters — your MGM240L Zigbee radio is intentional RF

11 Practical EMC Checklist

Use this checklist for every board you design:

✗ Common Mistakes

  • No ground plane or broken ground plane
  • Routing signals over plane splits
  • Decoupling caps far from IC pins
  • Long traces on clock/high-speed signals
  • 90° corners on RF/high-speed traces
  • No copper keepout around antenna
  • Mixing HV and LV traces (your RelaySwitch!)
  • No snubber on relay coil
  • Single-point grounding for mixed-signal
  • Ignoring return current paths

✓ Best Practices

  • Solid, unbroken ground plane on layer 2
  • 100nF decap ≤2mm from every power pin
  • Ferrite beads on SMPS output
  • 3W spacing between sensitive traces
  • Copper-free zone around antenna (12.5mm×5mm for MGM240L)
  • HV/LV isolation ≥2.5mm with slot
  • Snubber (RC or flyback diode) on relay coils
  • Short, direct crystal traces
  • Via stitching around board edges
  • Common-mode choke on Ethernet/USB
Quick self-test question for any trace: "Where does the return current for this signal flow?" If you can't answer it immediately, you probably have an EMI problem on that signal.
