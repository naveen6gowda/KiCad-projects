Summary of what's happening:
• I1→I2 carries full load current (up to 10A per relay) through thick pads
• E1→V1P and E2→V1N are sense-only connections — zero load current flows through them
• The HLW8012 measures the differential voltage: V1P − V1N = I_load × 1mΩ
• R10 is no longer needed — it was incorrectly bridging L to V1P. Remove it.
• The voltage divider (R4-R8, R9) for V2P stays unchanged — it correctly senses mains voltage

Alternative simpler approach: If you don't want Kelvin sensing, replace R18 WSK2512 with a standard 2-pin Device:R (1mΩ, 2512 footprint) in series with the live line. Then connect V1P and V1N to each side of that resistor. This eliminates E1/E2 complexity.
