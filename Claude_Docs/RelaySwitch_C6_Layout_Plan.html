⚠️ THE CORE PROBLEM: Will Everything Fit?

Your board is 50 × 45mm = 2,250 mm² total. Here's the math:

  • TOP side HV zone (72% = ~32mm height × 50mm): 1,600 mm² available → THT components need ~1,190 mm² + routing clearance
  • TOP side LV zone (28% = ~13mm height × 50mm): 650 mm² → J2 + J5 + J6 need ~52 mm² — plenty of room
  • BOTTOM side LV zone: 650 mm² available → SMD components need ~500 mm² — fits with careful placement
  • BOTTOM side HV zone: Reserved for GND pour + THT pin soldering only

✅ VERDICT: It DOES fit — but ONLY with the optimized layout shown above. The key changes from your original SVGs:

🔧 Critical Layout Changes (vs. your current design)

01

Move ALL connectors to ONE edge

Place J1, J3, J4 along the top edge in a row. This eliminates wasted space from having connectors on the bottom edge (your original SVG) — connectors at two edges creates a routing nightmare on a 45mm board. All screw terminals pointing outward from the same edge makes wiring much easier in an Indian switchboard.

02

HLK-2M05 directly below J1

Place U1 (HLK) in the top-left quadrant, immediately below J1. This minimizes AC mains trace length from L/N pins → HLK input. Keep F1 fuse and RV1 MOV between J1 and HLK. The R10 shunt goes between J1/N and HLK to intercept mains current for metering.

03

K1 and K2 side-by-side, right of HLK

Both relays in the top-right quadrant, horizontally aligned. This keeps relay output pins close to J3/J4 load connectors. Short, wide traces from relay contacts to screw terminals = lower resistance, better thermal performance.

04

Middle band = HV routing only

The ~14mm band between Row 2 (components) and the isolation barrier is pure routing space for wide AC traces (1.5mm+ for 10A). No components here — just traces: J1→F1→HLK, J1→K1→J3, J1→K2→J4. This is where your 230V tracks run.

05

J5, J6 on right edge, LV zone

Move switch inputs to the right edge of the LV zone, vertically stacked. This keeps them physically separated from HV, close to the ESP32 GPIO pins on the bottom side, and accessible for wall switch wiring.

06

ESP32-C6 centered on bottom LV zone

The XIAO module goes dead center of the LV zone on B.Cu. Castellated pads solder directly to copper. All supporting SMD components (U3, U4, Q1, Q2, HLW8012, resistors, caps) radiate outward from the ESP32 in the remaining LV area.

07

HLW8012 on bottom-left of LV zone

Place U5 (HLW8012) at the left side of B.Cu LV zone, directly below where R10 shunt is on the top side. Use vias to bring the current sense signal from R10 (F.Cu) down to HLW8012 (B.Cu). Voltage divider resistors (R4, R6, R7, R8, R13) cluster around U5.

08

J2 programming header — left edge LV zone

4-pin header for TX/RX/3V3/GND on the left edge of the LV zone. This gives easy access during development without interfering with the switch wiring on the right side.

✅ KiCad Placement Sequence

Follow this exact order in the PCB editor for best results:

  1. Draw board outline — 50mm wide × 45mm tall on Edge.Cuts
  2. Place J1, J3, J4 — flush against top edge, left-to-right. Lock positions (right-click → Lock)
  3. Place U1 (HLK-2M05) — below J1, top-left quadrant
  4. Place K1, K2 — right of HLK, aligned to top row
  5. Place F1, RV1, R10 — between J1 and HLK (F.Cu)
  6. Flip to B.Cu — Place U2 (ESP32-C6) centered in LV zone
  7. Place U5 (HLW8012) — bottom-left of LV zone on B.Cu
  8. Place U3, U4, Q1, Q2 — between HLW8012 and ESP32 on B.Cu
  9. Place J5, J6 — right edge, LV zone (F.Cu, pads through to B.Cu)
  10. Place J2 — left edge, LV zone (F.Cu)
  11. Scatter passives — close to their parent ICs, all on B.Cu
  12. Route HV traces first — wide 1.5mm+ traces on F.Cu only
  13. Route LV signals — 0.2mm traces on B.Cu only, staying below isolation line
  14. Pour GND — full B.Cu pour, HV GND pour on F.Cu HV zone (separated from LV GND)

🔴 Critical Safety Rules for Layout

  • 3mm minimum clearance between HV and LV traces/copper — this is your isolation barrier
  • No LV traces on F.Cu above the isolation line — F.Cu HV zone is for mains traces only
  • No LV traces on B.Cu above the isolation line — only GND pour with THT pads
  • HV mains traces: minimum 1.5mm width for 10A, 2mm preferred
  • Route voltage divider from mains (for HLW8012 VP) through series 470kΩ resistors — this is your safe isolation path for power metering
  • Optocoupler U3/U4 provide galvanic isolation for relay control — maintain 3mm clearance across their package
  • Place slot cutouts on the isolation barrier line if your fabricator supports them — adds mechanical creepage distance

💡 Space-Saving Tips

  • If still tight: consider 3.81mm pitch screw terminals instead of 5.08mm — saves ~6mm across J1+J3+J4
  • Use spring terminals (e.g., Wago 2060 series) — smaller footprint than traditional screw types
  • R10 shunt (2512) can go under the HLK module if there's vertical clearance — the HLK is 15mm tall, R10 is only ~1mm
  • Test points can be via-in-pad — no separate TP footprints needed, saves ~3mm²
  • Consider removing mounting holes — at 45×50mm, the board will be held by the switchboard module enclosure, not screws
