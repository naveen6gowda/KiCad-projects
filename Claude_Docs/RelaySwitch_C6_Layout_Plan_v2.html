🔍 What Was Wrong & What Changed

🔴 Problem 1: HLK-2M05 overlapping XIAO ESP32-C6

In your screenshot, the HLK-2M05 (34×20mm, 15mm tall THT module) was placed in the center of the board, directly above where the ESP32 needs to be. Since the ESP32 uses THT pin headers (not flat castellated SMD), both components are on the same side and their bodies collide.

Fix: Move HLK-2M05 to the top-left quadrant, directly below J1 (AC mains input). This places it entirely within the HV zone, with short mains traces to J1. The ESP32 goes in the bottom-center LV zone, well clear of the HLK.

🔴 Problem 2: K2 doesn't fit inside the board

K2 was placed at the bottom-right of the board, outside or overlapping the board outline. This happened because the HLK was taking up center space, pushing K2 outward.

Fix: With HLK moved to top-left, K1 and K2 sit side-by-side in the top-center and top-right, directly below J3/J4 load connectors. Each relay is only 12.5×7.5mm — two relays side by side need ~27mm width, which fits easily in the 35mm remaining after HLK.

🔴 Problem 3: RV1, F1, R10 placed outside the board

Your screenshot shows the MOV, fuse, and shunt resistor floating outside the board outline to the left.

Fix: F1 (1206 SMD) and R10 (2512 SMD) are small — they go in the top-right area near J1, between the connectors and the relays. RV1 (MOV disc ~10mm) fits next to F1. These are in the HV mains current path, so they need to be close to J1 anyway.

⚠️ Problem 4: ESP32 footprint assumptions

My previous layout assumed castellated SMD (flat, 1mm tall on B.Cu). Your actual footprint uses THT pin headers, meaning:

  • The module sits ~8.5mm above the PCB on the TOP side
  • Pin header through-holes occupy two rows of pads on B.Cu (solder side)
  • The module body blocks placement of SMD components underneath it on B.Cu
  • The footprint is effectively ~25.4 × 17.8mm (pin-to-pin outer edge)

Fix: ESP32 is treated as a TOP-side THT component in the LV zone. All small SMD passives go on B.Cu outside the ESP32 header pad area.

⚠️ The Tight Spot: Row 2 (HLK + Relays)

HLK-2M05 at 34×20mm is the biggest single component. With K1+K2 (each 12.5×7.5mm), Row 2 needs:

34mm (HLK width) + 2mm gap + 12.5mm (K1) + 2mm gap + 12.5mm (K2) = 63mm

But your board is only 50mm wide. So they can't all fit in a single horizontal row!

Solution: Stack them — HLK occupies the left 34mm width × 20mm height. K1 and K2 go to the right of HLK, vertically stacked (K1 above K2), using only 12.5mm width × 17mm height for both relays. This fits: 34 + 2 + 12.5 = 48.5mm ✅

✅ Key Principle: Vertical Stacking of K1 + K2

The critical insight that makes this fit: K1 and K2 are stacked vertically (not horizontally) to the right of the HLK. Each relay is 12.5mm wide × 7.5mm deep. Stacked: 12.5mm wide × 17mm deep (including 2mm gap). This uses only 12.5mm of board width, leaving 50 - 34 - 2 - 12.5 = 1.5mm — very tight but mechanically feasible. If you need more clearance, shift the HLK 1-2mm to the left (its courtyard may extend slightly over the board edge on the left — check if your fab house allows 0.5mm overhang for edge-mount).

💡 Alternative: If it still doesn't fit

If the HLK + 2 relays + protection components are still too tight in the HV zone, consider these options:

  • Switch to HLK-PM01 (25×20mm) instead of HLK-2M05 (34×20mm) — saves 10mm width! It's 5V/0.6W instead of 5V/2W, but the ESP32 + 2 relays only need ~0.6W total, so it's sufficient.
  • Use 3.81mm pitch terminals instead of 5.08mm — saves ~4mm across J1+J3+J4.
  • Increase board to 55×45mm — adds 5mm width, solving all fit issues with comfortable margins.
  • Use SMD relay (e.g., Omron G6K-2F-Y) if height clearance allows — frees top-side space.
