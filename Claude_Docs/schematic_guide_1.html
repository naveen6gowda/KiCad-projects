AC Mains Input & Fuse Protection

Sheet 1 of 8 — Power Entry Point
⚡ HIGH VOLTAGEPOWER ENTRY
SHEET 1 — AC TERMINAL J1 + FUSE F1 + MOV RV1 Mains Entry
⚡ DANGER — 230V MAINSNever touch this circuit powered. All work at this voltage requires an isolation transformer for testing.
// STEP-BY-STEP CONNECTIONS
1
Place J1 (KF350 3-pin) at the board edge. Screw openings must face outward for cable entry.
2
Run a 2mm trace from J1 Pin 1 (L) to one leg of fuse holder F1.
3
Other leg of F1 → net LIVE_FUSED. This is the protected live line going to everything else.
4
J1 Pin 2 (N) → net NEUTRAL directly (no fuse on neutral).
5
J1 Pin 3 (PE) → chassis ground. Optional if plastic enclosure.
6
(Opt.) RV1 MOV 275VAC between L and N at the terminal block for surge protection.
// COMPONENTS
J1: KF350 / KF301 3P 3.5mm
F1: 5×20mm holder + 10A glass fuse
RV1: 07D271K 275VAC 10mm MOV

Trace: ≥2.0mm mains
Clearance HV→LV: ≥3.0mm
WIRE COLOR CODE
LIVE — Brown wire
NEUTRAL — Blue wire
EARTH — Green/Yellow

HLK-PM01 Isolated Power Supply

Sheet 2 of 8 — AC→DC 5V/600mA
⚡ HIGH VOLTAGE INPUT+5V OUTPUT
SHEET 2 — HLK-PM01 + BULK CAPS C1 C2 U2
💡 HLK-PM01 WIRINGOnly 4 wires: L, N (AC input) and +Vo, −Vo (DC output). The module handles rectification and isolation internally.
// STEP-BY-STEP CONNECTIONS
1
Connect net LIVE_FUSED to HLK-PM01 L pin.
2
Connect net NEUTRAL to HLK-PM01 N pin.
3
From +Vo pin → connect to C1 positive leg (100µF/16V electrolytic).
4
Add C2 100nF ceramic cap in parallel with C1 (same two nodes). Place both caps within 3mm of the module pins.
5
Connect −Vo pin and both cap negative legs to GND.
6
Label the positive output net +5V. This fans out to AMS1117, relay coils, PC817, and HLW8012.
// HLK-PM01 PINOUT (SIP-4)
Pin 1: L (AC Live input)
Pin 2: N (AC Neutral input)
Pin 3: +Vo (5V DC output)
Pin 4: −Vo (GND output)

Max load: 600mA
Isolation: ~3kVAC

AMS1117-3.3 LDO Regulator

Sheet 3 of 8 — 5V → 3.3V for MCU & Logic
LOW VOLTAGE+3.3V OUTPUT
SHEET 3 — AMS1117-3.3 SOT-223 + CAPS U3
// STEP-BY-STEP CONNECTIONS
1
Connect +5V net to AMS1117 IN pin (left of SOT-223). Add Cin 10µF electrolytic here (+ to +5V).
2
Connect AMS1117 GND pin and the big bottom thermal TAB both to GND. The tab is a heat sink — must be soldered to GND pad.
3
From OUT pin add Cout 10µF electrolytic (+ to output side).
4
Also add Cbp 100nF ceramic in parallel with Cout. Place both caps within 2mm of the OUT pin.
5
Label output net +3.3V. Connect to ESP32 3V3 pin, all pull-up resistors, and PC817 input-side logic.
// KEY NOTES
TAB on SOT-223 bottom must connect to GND copper pour for heat dissipation.

Max current: 1A — covers ESP32 WiFi peak (~200mA) + all logic.

Place AMS1117 close to ESP32 to minimise 3.3V trace length.

LOW VOLTAGEMCU CORE
SHEET 4 — ESP32-C6 SUPERMINI PIN MAP U1
📡 ANTENNA KEEPOUTPCB antenna is on the USB-C end (left side on board). Keep ALL copper, vias and traces at least 5mm away from that edge on both layers.

PC817 Optocoupler — Isolation Stage

Sheet 5 of 8 — MCU ↔ Relay 5000V Isolation
5000V ISOLATIONLV INPUT SIDE
SHEET 5 — PC817 × 2 OPTOCOUPLERS CH1 & CH2 U4, U5
⟵⟶ ISOLATION BARRIERThe dashed purple line marks the isolation boundary. Never connect left-side (LV) and right-side (collector/emitter) nodes together. The opto provides 5000V isolation between the ESP32 and relay drive circuit.
// STEP-BY-STEP CONNECTIONS
1
Connect net RELAY_CH1 through 1kΩ R1 to PC817 U4 pin 1 (Anode).
2
Connect U4 pin 2 (Cathode) directly to GND.
3
Connect 10kΩ R2 between +5V and U4 pin 4 (Collector). This is the pull-up.
4
Connect U4 pin 3 (Emitter) to GND.
5
The collector node (junction of R2 bottom + pin 4) becomes CTRL_CH1 → goes to S8050 base (Sheet 6).
6
Repeat steps 1–5 exactly for CH2 using R3, R4, and U5, producing net CTRL_CH2.
// LOGIC LEVELS
GPIO LOW (0V) → LED ON → transistor saturates → CTRL goes LOW → relay turns ON

GPIO HIGH (3.3V) → LED OFF → transistor off → CTRL pulled HIGH by R2 → relay turns OFF

Active-LOW relay logic.

S8050 Relay Driver + HF3FF Relay + Flyback

Sheet 6 of 8 — Coil Driver, Relay Contacts & Protection
⚡ HV CONTACTSLV COIL DRIVE
SHEET 6 — S8050 Q1 + HF3FF RL1 + D1 1N4007 (ONE CHANNEL — REPEAT FOR CH2) Q1, RL1, D1
⚡ HV CONTACTS — 230VACThe relay NO/COM/NC contacts carry 230VAC at up to 10A. Maintain 3mm clearance from all LV circuits.
// STEP-BY-STEP CONNECTIONS
1
Connect CTRL_CH1 through 1kΩ R5 to S8050 Q1 Base pin (middle pin of TO-92 flat face).
2
Q1 Emitter (left pin) → GND.
3
Q1 Collector (right pin) → relay RL1 Pin 1 (Coil +).
4
Relay Pin 2 (Coil −) → +5V.
5
Place D1 1N4007 across coil pins: Cathode (band end) → Pin2 (+5V side), Anode → Pin1 (collector side). Protects against back-EMF spike.
6
Relay Pin 3 (COM) → LIVE_FUSED.
7
Relay Pin 5 (NO) → Load terminal L output of J2.
8
NEUTRAL → Load terminal N output of J2 directly (never switched).
9
Repeat all steps for CH2 using Q2, RL2, D2, and J3.

HLW8012 Energy Monitoring

Sheet 7 of 8 — Voltage, Current & Power Measurement
⚡ MAINS CONNECTEDENERGY METER
SHEET 7 — HLW8012 / BL0937 + SHUNT + VOLTAGE DIVIDER U6, R_sh, R7–R9
⚡ MAINS ON HLW8012 PINSThe VP, IP+, and IP− pins are directly connected to mains voltage. These pins and their resistors must maintain 5mm minimum clearance from all LV components.
// STEP-BY-STEP CONNECTIONS
1
HLW8012 VCC (pin 5) → +5V. Add 100nF bypass cap close to this pin to GND.
2
HLW8012 GND (pin 8) → GND.
3
IP+ (pin 2) → one side of 1mΩ shunt R_sh. Other side of shunt → LIVE_FUSED. The shunt measures load current.
4
IP− (pin 3) → other shunt terminal → relay COM. Add 10nF across IP+ and IP−.
5
VP (pin 4) → mid-point of voltage divider: R7 (470kΩ) + R8 (470kΩ) in series from LIVE, R9 (1kΩ) to GND. Add 10nF/630V across VP to GND.
6
CF (pin 6) → net HLW_CF → GPIO20. Add 10kΩ pull-up to 3.3V.
7
CF1 (pin 7) → net HLW_CF1 → GPIO21. Add 10kΩ pull-up to 3.3V.
8
SEL (pin 1) → net HLW_SEL → GPIO22 (no pull-up needed, ESP32 drives this).
